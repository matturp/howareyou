How are you?

Mental health isn't always easy to talk about. Putting feelings into words can be inaccurate and frustrating. This is a tool that aims to help translate mental health into something tangible that can be used to help understand our own mental health, track our progress, and facilitate open discussion.

Pictogram

  1. Scroll down to generate your mental health pictogram.
  2. In the box below are some descriptors for the variables if you need some clarification.
  3. Click the download button when you're done to get your pictogram.
  4. DM the image to @howareyoumh on Instagram with a short text response to: 'How are you?'
  5. All submissions are posted completely anonymously.
  • Mood: Your mood in general. (Bad - Neutral - Good)
  • Interest: How much you've been maintining interest in things you usually like. (Disinterested - Neutral - Interested)
  • Energy: Your physical energy level. (Tired - Neutral - Buzzing)
  • Appetite: Appetite for food and drink. (Undereating - Neutral - Overeating)
  • Self worth: Feelings about your sense of worth. (Low - Neutral - High)
  • Physical pain: Presence/intensity of any physical pain. (Absent - Extreme pain)
  • Irritability: Degree of recent irritability. (Not irritabile at all - Very irritable)
  • Anxiety: Presence/intensity of anxiety. (Calm - Extremely anxious)
  • Insomnia: Difficulty with sleeping. (Yes/No)
  • Suicidal ideation: Having thoughts about death or dying. (Yes/No)
  • Brain fog: Difficulty concentrating or remembering things. (Yes/No)
